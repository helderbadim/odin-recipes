Bucatini all`Amatriciana

Bucatini all'amatriciana is a classic Italian pasta dish originating from the town of Amatrice in the Lazio region of Italy. It's a simple yet flavorful recipe that typically features pasta tossed in a sauce made with tomatoes, guanciale (cured pork cheek), Pecorino Romano cheese, and sometimes chili flakes for a bit of heat.

Ingredients for spaghetti all'amatriciana for 4 people:

  • 350 grams of bucatini
  • 150 grams of guanciale or pancetta diced
  • 300 grams of canned tomato, crushed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Grated Pecorino Romano, for serving

How to prepare:

  1. Heat a large pot of salted water over high heat and bring it to a boil. Cook the bucatini according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced guanciale or pancetta and cook until it's crispy and golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Remove the cooked guanciale or pancetta from the skillet and set it aside on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess fat.
  4. In the same skillet, add a bit more olive oil if needed. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and saut√© until the onion is soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes and red pepper flakes to the skillet. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Allow the sauce to simmer over medium-low heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  6. Once the sauce has thickened, add the cooked guanciale or pancetta back into the skillet and stir to combine.
  7. Drain the cooked spaghetti and add it to the skillet with the sauce. Toss everything together until the pasta is evenly coated with the sauce.
  8. Serve the spaghetti all'amatriciana hot, topped with a generous sprinkle of grated Pecorino Romano cheese. Buon appetito!
